Frequently Asked Questions

Kratom, or Mitragyna speciosa, is an evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. Its leaves have been used in traditional medicine for generations. Kratom is popular in various forms such as powders, capsules, and extracts, and is known for its potential effects on well-being. Different strains, including green, red, and white vein varieties, are harvested at different maturity levels, with specific qualities and alkaloid compositions.

Kratom works primarily through its alkaloids, which interact with opioid receptors in the brain, leading to various effects. Users report enhanced mood, increased energy, and in some cases, relief from discomfort. The impact can vary depending on the strain and dosage. For example, kratom green horn is often noted for providing a balanced effect, while Indonesian kratom may offer varying levels of stimulation.

Kratom does not have a strict expiration date but can lose potency over time. Proper storage in a cool, dry place can extend its freshness. However, exposure to moisture, heat, or air can lead to degradation. For optimal use, consider consuming kratom within 1 to 2 years of purchase, and always check for signs of mold or unusual smells.

Driving on kratom raises safety concerns. While many users report enhanced focus and energy, kratom can also cause drowsiness in some individuals. It’s essential to understand how your body reacts to kratom before operating a vehicle.

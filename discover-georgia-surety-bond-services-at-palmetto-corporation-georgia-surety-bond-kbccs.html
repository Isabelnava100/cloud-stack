A Georgia surety bond serves as a financial guarantee that a party will fulfill their obligations under a contract. Essentially, it involves three parties: the principal (the party required to obtain the bond), the obligee (the party requesting the bond), and the surety (the company issuing the bond). If the principal fails to meet their obligations, the surety pays out the bond amount to the obligee, who may then pursue repayment from the principal. Palmetto Surety Corporation provides clear guidance on navigating this process.

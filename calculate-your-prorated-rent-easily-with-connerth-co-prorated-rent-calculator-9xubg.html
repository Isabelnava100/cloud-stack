A prorated rent calculator is a tool used to calculate the amount of rent that should be paid for a partial month of occupancy. This is especially useful when a tenant moves in or out mid-month, allowing for an accurate rental agreement based on the number of days the property is occupied during that month.

To calculate prorated rent, use the formula: (Monthly Rent ÷ Days in Month) x Days Occupied. For example, if the rent is $1,200 and there are 30 days in the month, the daily rate would be $40. If a tenant occupied the unit for 10 days, the prorated rent would be $400 ($40 x 10 days). This ensures fair rental agreement calculations for all tenants.

Using a prorated rent calculator is essential for ensuring fairness in situations where tenants move in or out mid-month. It provides peace of mind to both landlords and tenants, ensuring rental agreement calculations accurately reflect the actual usage of the property and avoid disputes over payments.

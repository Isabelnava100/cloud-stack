Bail bonds are financial agreements provided by licensed bail bond companies to secure the release of an individual awaiting trial. They are essential for those who cannot afford to pay the full bail amount set by the court. When you use a bail bond service like AffordABail, you pay a percentage of the total bail amount (often around 10%) to the bail bondsman, who then provides the full amount to the court on your behalf. This allows for a quicker release from custody, enabling individuals to prepare for their court hearings while remaining free.

Felony bail bonds in Tennessee operate similarly to other types of bail bonds. When someone is charged with a felony, the court will set a bail amount based on the severity of the charge and the individual's criminal history. If the bail amount is too high for the accused to pay, they can seek a felony bail bond from a licensed bail bondsman like AffordABail. By paying a non-refundable premium to the bondsman, the bondsman then posts the bail for the full amount, allowing the individual to be released until their court date.

What happens if a person does not appear in court after being released on a bail bond?
If an individual fails to appear in court after being released on a bail bond, the court will issue a bench warrant for their arrest. This situation can result in serious consequences, including the forfeiture of the bail bond premium paid to the bondsman. The bail bond company, like AffordABail, will also be responsible for the full bail amount, which may lead them to take action to locate the person and ensure their return to court. It’s crucial to adhere to all court appearances to avoid complications and additional legal ramifications.

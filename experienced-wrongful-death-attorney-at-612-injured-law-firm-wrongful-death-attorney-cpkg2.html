Frequently Asked Questions

A wrongful death claim in Minnesota allows the surviving family members of a deceased person to seek compensation for their losses caused by someone's negligence or wrongful act. This legal action can address various damages, including funeral expenses, lost income, and emotional suffering. Minnesota law recognizes that when a person's life is cut short due to the wrongful conduct of another, the affected family deserves justice and financial support. At 612 Injured, our experienced wrongful death attorneys understand the complexities involved in these claims and are dedicated to helping families navigate this difficult process.

A wrongful death claim typically starts with the appointment of a personal representative for the deceased's estate. This individual files the claim on behalf of the surviving family members. The process involves investigating the circumstances of the death, gathering evidence, and presenting the case either through settlement negotiations or in court. At 612 Injured, our team provides comprehensive support throughout this journey, ensuring that every aspect of the claim is meticulously handled to maximize compensation and secure justice for the grieving family.

In Minnesota, the law allows specific parties to file a wrongful death claim. Typically, the deceased person's spouse, children, or parents have the right to bring forth the claim. If there are no eligible family members, a personal representative can be appointed to file on behalf of the estate. At 612 Injured, we can guide you through the process of determining eligibility and help you understand the rights of each family member in pursuing a Minnesota wrongful death claim.

In a Minnesota wrongful death claim, families may seek various damages, including economic and non-economic losses. Economic damages can cover funeral and burial expenses, medical costs incurred prior to death, and loss of the deceased's income. Non-economic damages address pain and suffering, loss of companionship, and emotional distress experienced by the surviving family members. Our knowledgeable wrongful death attorneys at 612 Injured are committed to ensuring that every aspect of your claim is represented, helping you recover the compensation necessary to cope with your loss.

In Minnesota, the statute of limitations for filing a wrongful death claim is typically three years from the date of the deceased's passing. It is essential to act promptly, as delaying could jeopardize your ability to seek compensation. Additionally, various factors may affect the timeline, so consulting with a qualified wrongful death attorney at 612 Injured is crucial to ensure you meet all necessary deadlines and effectively navigate the complexities of your case.
